<p><ul><li>Bisphosphonates</li><li>Parathyroid Hormone Therapy</li><li>Calcitonin</li><li>Selective Estrogen Inhibitors Modulator (SERM)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Osteoporosis treatment includes several market types. Bisphosphonates are widely used to prevent bone loss by inhibiting osteoclast activity. Parathyroid hormone therapy stimulates bone formation and increases bone density. Calcitonin, a hormone, helps regulate calcium levels and is used to reduce pain from fractures. Selective Estrogen Receptor Modulators (SERMs) mimic estrogen's beneficial effects on bone without some estrogen-related risks, offering a targeted approach to maintaining bone density. Together, these treatments help manage osteoporosis effectively.</p></p>
