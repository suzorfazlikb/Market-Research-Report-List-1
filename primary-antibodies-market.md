<p><p>The primary antibodies market is segmented into two main types: polyclonal and monoclonal antibodies. Polyclonal antibodies are derived from multiple B-cell lineages, providing a diverse range of antibody specificities, making them versatile for various applications. In contrast, monoclonal antibodies are produced from a single B-cell clone, ensuring high specificity and consistency, often used in diagnostic and therapeutic applications. Both types play critical roles in research, diagnostics, and therapeutics, catering to different research needs and clinical requirements.</p></p>
