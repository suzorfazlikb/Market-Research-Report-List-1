<p><p>The Pisco market is characterized by two primary types: Peruvian Pisco and Chilean Pisco, each reflecting distinct production methods and regulations. Peruvian Pisco is crafted exclusively from specific grape varieties, undergoes a single distillation process, and must be bottled unaged, emphasizing purity and character. In contrast, Chilean Pisco allows for a wider range of grapes, permits multiple distillations, and can be aged in wooden barrels, resulting in varied flavor profiles. Both markets celebrate their unique heritage and attract diverse consumer preferences globally.</p></p>
